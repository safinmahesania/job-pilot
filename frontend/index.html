Scoring

        


          

            

              
Score through the provider chain

              

                Uses Cerebras / Gemini instead of local Ollama — far faster, and a better
                judgement of fit. Ollama stays at the end of the chain, so an offline machine
                still works. Turn this off to pin scoring to local only.
              

            

            
          


          

            
Learning from your decisions

            

              Every job you save or dismiss teaches the scoring what you actually want. The
              ones it scored highly and you dismissed anyway are the most useful of all — those
              are its mistakes, and it is shown them.
            


            

            
          

        

      


      
      

        
Privacy

        

          Writing a cover letter genuinely needs your background — that's the point of it.
          It does not need your phone number.
        


        

          

          

            

              
Follow job links to recover descriptions

              

                Alert-email links are tracking redirects. Following one lets LinkedIn or Indeed
                know the message was engaged with — but you were going to click it yourself
                anyway, from this same machine. Turn it off and every alert-email job stays
                unscored, because scoring needs a description.

Import jobs

        

          Bring in postings the fetch pipeline doesn't cover. Jobs whose description
          can be recovered are scored as usual; the rest land in
          Unscored for you to triage by hand —
          never given a number the model had no basis for.

Paste a job posting

              description. Anything it can't find is left blank rather than guessed.
            

            
            
          


          
          

            
Job-alert emails

            

              Set up a saved search on LinkedIn and Indeed — they email you the results.
              Export the email and hand it over.
            


            

              
              
                JobPilot has no mail credentials and no IMAP client. There is no path
                from this app to your mailbox. An app password can read an entire account, and
                no amount of careful code makes that safe to hold — so it isn't held. JobPilot
                reads the file you give it, and nothing else.
              
            


            

              In your mail client: open the alert → Save as / Download message
              (.eml or .html).
              Select as many as you like — they're imported one after another so you can
              watch the queue. Or drop them into
              data/mail_drop/ and read them in one go.
